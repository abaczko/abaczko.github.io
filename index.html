When I look into the sky ...

As an astronomer, when I look to the sky I wonder “How long did it take all this light to reach me?”

For the moon, it took 1 minute and 20 seconds. For “our” star, the sun, it took 8 minutes and 20 seconds. If we look towards the center of our milky way we see light that was emitted 26,000 years ago. This is because the center of our home galaxy is about 8 kilo parsec away (an astronomical measurement for distance). This corresponds to about 26,000 light years. These are incredibly large numbers, even though we are still inside the milky way.

The astronomical objects I am most fascinated by are at even larger distances. Those are located at least 60 Million light years away, which is still regarded as our local universe – our neighborhood, in a way. Fascinatingly enough, I am not interested in the ordinary galaxy, but the ones whose innermost center manage to out-shine the whole star population. These are called Active Galaxies and they are extremely powerful.
